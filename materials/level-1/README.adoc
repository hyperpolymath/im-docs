= Level 1: Evidence Mapper
:toc:
:toclevels: 3

**Capability:** Skeptical Journalist → Evidence Mapper

**Time Investment:** 4-8 hours

**Deliverable:** One claim mapped with PROMPT-evaluated evidence

== Overview

Welcome to Level 1 of the Wayfinder learning pathway. This level transforms you from a skeptical journalist into an evidence mapper who can systematically evaluate claims using the PROMPT framework.

**What You'll Learn:**
- Apply the PROMPT framework to a single claim
- Map evidence ecosystems systematically
- Score evidence across six dimensions
- Make epistemic reasoning transparent

**What You'll Produce:**
- One fully mapped claim with evaluated evidence
- Professional output you can use in your work immediately
- Foundation for advanced Wayfinder capabilities

== The PROMPT Framework

Every piece of evidence is evaluated across six dimensions:

[cols="1,3,2"]
|===
|Dimension |Question |What to Check

|**P**rovenance
|Who said it? What's their track record?
|Author credentials, previous accuracy, conflicts of interest

|**R**elevance
|Does this actually address the claim?
|Logical connection, directly vs. tangentially related

|**O**bjectivity
|What biases exist? Are they disclosed?
|Funding sources, ideological positions, transparency

|**M**ethods
|How was evidence gathered? Is it sound?
|Sample size, methodology, peer review, replicability

|**P**erspective
|What viewpoints are represented? What's missing?
|Diverse voices, affected communities, dissenting views

|**T**imeliness
|Is this current? Has it been superseded?
|Publication date, more recent evidence, ongoing developments
|===

== Learning Pathway

=== Step 1: Choose Your Claim (30 minutes)

Pick one claim from your current reporting. Good starter claims:

**✓ Good:**
- "Electric vehicles reduce carbon emissions by 40%"
- "Remote work increases productivity"
- "Vaccine X is 95% effective against disease Y"

**✗ Too Broad:**
- "Climate change is real"
- "Technology affects society"
- "Healthcare needs reform"

**Criteria:**
- Specific and testable
- Relevant to your beat
- Has multiple evidence sources
- You're genuinely uncertain about

=== Step 2: Gather Evidence (1-2 hours)

Find 5-10 pieces of evidence for your claim. Include:

- **Primary sources** (original research, official documents)
- **Expert analysis** (credentials matter!)
- **Statistical data** (with methodology)
- **Contradictory evidence** (this is CRITICAL)

**Resources:**
- Academic databases (JSTOR, PubMed)
- Government reports
- Think tank studies
- Investigative journalism
- Expert interviews

=== Step 3: Apply PROMPT Framework (2-3 hours)

For each piece of evidence, score all six PROMPT dimensions.

**Scoring Scale:**
- **0.0-0.3:** Low quality (serious issues)
- **0.4-0.6:** Medium quality (some concerns)
- **0.7-0.9:** High quality (reliable)
- **1.0:** Gold standard (rare!)

Use the `PROMPT-Worksheet.adoc` template to document your analysis.

=== Step 4: Map the Evidence Ecosystem (1-2 hours)

Create a visual or textual map showing:

- How evidence pieces relate
- Where they converge (agreement)
- Where they diverge (disagreement)
- What's missing (gaps)

Use the `Evidence-Map-Template.adoc` to structure your map.

=== Step 5: Write Your Transparent Summary (1 hour)

Summarize what you learned:

1. **What we know:** Evidence with high PROMPT scores
2. **What's uncertain:** Areas of disagreement or low scores
3. **What's missing:** Gaps in evidence
4. **Confidence level:** Overall epistemic status

**Key Principle:** Show your reasoning, don't hide it.

== Deliverables

=== Required

1. **PROMPT Worksheet:** All evidence scored
2. **Evidence Map:** Visual or textual representation
3. **Transparent Summary:** 300-500 words

=== Optional

1. **Share with 1-2 peers** for feedback
2. **Publish as blog post** (transparency builds trust)
3. **Use in current story** (immediate professional value)

== Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Cherry-picking evidence**
- **Problem:** Only including evidence that supports your prior belief
- **Fix:** Actively seek contradictory evidence, score it fairly

**Mistake 2: Confusing Relevance with Quality**
- **Problem:** High-quality evidence that doesn't address the claim
- **Fix:** Relevance is a separate dimension - be honest

**Mistake 3: Ignoring Timeliness**
- **Problem:** Using outdated evidence without acknowledging it
- **Fix:** Check for more recent research that might supersede

**Mistake 4: False Precision**
- **Problem:** Scoring 0.87 when you mean "pretty good"
- **Fix:** Use ranges (0.7-0.9) if uncertain

== Completion Checklist

Before moving to Level 2, verify:

- ☐ Claim is specific and testable
- ☐ 5-10 evidence pieces gathered
- ☐ All evidence scored across all 6 PROMPT dimensions
- ☐ Contradictory evidence included and fairly evaluated
- ☐ Evidence map shows relationships and gaps
- ☐ Transparent summary explains reasoning
- ☐ Deliverables are professionally usable

== Next Steps

**Level 2A: Process Documentarian** (8-12 hours)
- Document your methodology transparently
- Show sources contacted (responded, declined, unreachable)
- Expose editorial reasoning chains

**Level 2B: Uncertainty Communicator** (6-10 hours)
- Replace false certainty with honest uncertainty
- Use Dempster-Shafer intervals
- Communicate confidence levels effectively

**Choose based on your interests:**
- Methodologically-minded? → Level 2A
- Data/statistics-focused? → Level 2B
- Want both? Do either first, then the other
